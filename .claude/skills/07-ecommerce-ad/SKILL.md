## The 2-Second Hook Framework: E-Commerce Edition

The first 2 seconds determine if viewers scroll past or watch to purchase. These 10+ hook patterns work across platforms when generated with Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield.

### Hook 1: Product Drop with Dramatic Impact
**The Setup:** Dark, minimal background. Product descends with motion blur and dramatic lighting shift.
**Why It Works:** Instantly signals "premium" and "new arrival." Grabs attention in feed.
**Example:** Luxury watch falling into frame against black silk, catching light mid-drop.

### Hook 2: Satisfying Texture ASMR Close-Up
**The Setup:** Macro shot of product texture—fabric weave, metal grain, skin texture.
**Why It Works:** Triggers sensory engagement. Viewers pause to watch satisfying detail.
**Example:** Fingers sliding across velvet fabric, revealing product name beneath.

### Hook 3: Before/After Transformation
**The Setup:** Split-screen or quick transition showing problem → solved state.
**Why It Works:** Instantly communicates benefit. Answers "what's in it for me?"
**Example:** Dull skin → glowing skin (skincare). Messy desk → organized desk (organizer).

### Hook 4: "Stop Scrolling" Direct Address
**The Setup:** Model/creator looks directly at camera, hand gesture to camera.
**Why It Works:** Breaks the fourth wall. Feels personal and urgent.
**Example:** Influencer hand stop gesture + text overlay "Wait, this is life-changing."

### Hook 5: Unboxing Reveal
**The Setup:** Hands open premium packaging. Product emerges into light.
**Why It Works:** Taps into unboxing ASMR obsession. Shows quality through packaging.
**Example:** Gold-foil box opening to reveal luxury skincare bottle.

### Hook 6: Color/Variant Cascade
**The Setup:** Products line up, rotating to show color variants. Quick cuts.
**Why It Works:** Shows range. Viewers looking for "their color" stay watching.
**Example:** Lipstick lineup rotating through 12 shades in 2 seconds.

### Hook 7: Ingredient Explosion
**The Setup:** Product ingredients splinter outward—visual, dynamic, energetic.
**Why It Works:** Communicates "natural" or "premium ingredients" visually.
**Example:** Honey, oats, lavender explode outward from skincare jar center.

### Hook 8: Problem-Solution Snap
**The Setup:** Text overlay "Problem:" → quick cut → "Solution:" with product.
**Why It Works:** Direct, solution-focused. No fluff.
**Example:** "Can't find pants that fit" → product reveal with fit guarantee text.

### Hook 9: In-Hand Usage Tease
**The Setup:** Hands holding product, demonstrating key feature in 2 seconds.
**Why It Works:** Shows context immediately. "I see how this fits into my life."
**Example:** Phone case dropping, catching product, surviving impact intact.

### Hook 10: Lifestyle Aspiration Flash
**The Setup:** Quick cut to beautiful, relatable lifestyle scene with product integrated.
**Why It Works:** Sells the life, not the product. Emotional hook.
**Example:** Morning latte in premium mug, sunlight on face, serene kitchen.

### Hook 11: Scarcity/Urgency Signal
**The Setup:** Text overlay "Limited Drop" or "Only 500 Made" with product glow.
**Why It Works:** FOMO drives click-through and add-to-cart.
**Example:** Product with "48HR FLASH SALE" animated text, timer visible.

### Hook 12: Creator Reaction
**The Setup:** Creator unboxes, eyes light up, brief gasp or "oh my god" moment.
**Why It Works:** Authentic emotional response is contagious.
**Example:** Unboxing beauty box, creator's surprised expression, product reveal.

## E-Commerce Ad Philosophy: What Actually Sells

### Core Principles

**1. Hero Shots Are Non-Negotiable**
Every e-commerce ad needs a moment where the product is the star. Use Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield to render a pure, isolated product shot with perfect lighting. No distractions. This becomes your "money shot."

**2. Multi-Angle Storytelling**
Rotate the product. Show front, back, side, detail. Viewers need to "see themselves" owning the product. Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield excels at smooth 360 rotations.

**3. In-Context Usage**
Show product in real life. Not sterile product photography—genuine lifestyle integration. Person wearing the jacket. Coffee in the mug. Ring on the hand.

**4. Before/After Is Psychology Gold**
Not just transformation ads. Show: problem state → moment of realization → product solution → happy outcome. The journey matters.

**5. Quick-Cut Energy for Scroll Stoppers**
Social feeds reward high-energy cuts. 0.5-1 second cuts per shot. Build momentum. Use Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield's smooth transitions to maintain polish while cutting fast.

**6. Texture & Tactile Detail**
E-commerce buyers can't touch. Create tactile desire through close-ups. Fabric weave. Metal grain. Ingredient shine. Let Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield's photorealism do the heavy lifting.

**7. Lifestyle Aspiration, Not Just Function**
Don't just show what the product does. Show how it makes the buyer feel. Confident. Beautiful. Organized. Powerful. Healthy.

**8. Text Overlays That Convert**
Lazy copywriting kills ads. Use text strategically:
- Problem identification
- Key benefit callout
- Social proof (ratings, sold count)
- Pricing and offer
- CTA ("Shop Now," "Join Waitlist")

## Text & CTA Guide

### Strategic Text Overlay Placement

**0-2 Second Hook:**
- Problem statement or attention-grab phrase
- Size: Large, bold, readable on mobile
- Color: High contrast (white on dark, dark on light)
- Animation: Fade-in or pop effect

**Product Showcase (Middle):**
- Key benefit (e.g., "Waterproof," "All-Day Wear," "Eco-Friendly")
- Social proof (e.g., "⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 2,000+ Reviews," "Sold Out In 48 Hours")
- Feature callout (e.g., "Made in Italy," "Dermatologist Tested")

**Final 3 Seconds (CTA):**
- Price (if not previously shown): "$49.99" or "$49.99 → $34.99"
- Discount/offer: "30% OFF," "Free Shipping," "Limited Time"
- CTA button/text: "Shop Now," "Get It Today," "Learn More," "Join Waitlist"
- Urgency marker: "Only 12 Left," "Sale Ends Sunday," "This Week Only"

### CTA Animation Styles
- **Slide-in:** Text slides from left/right with bounce
- **Pop:** Text appears with scale effect and slight shake
- **Glow:** Text glows or pulses to draw eye
- **Underline:** Animated line appears underneath, draws attention
- **Button effect:** Text sits in clickable button shape, animates interaction state

### Font & Readability Rules
- Use sans-serif for maximum mobile readability
- Minimum 24pt for mobile viewing
- Max 8 words per text overlay
- High contrast ratio (AAA WCAG compliance)
- Avoid thin, delicate fonts—bold is better

### Social Proof Copy Examples
- "⭐ 4.9 stars (5,847 reviews)"
- "👥 50K+ customers worldwide"
- "✅ Trending on TikTok"
- "🏆 Award-winning design"
- "🔥 Viral bestseller"

- **Warm & Cozy:** Home, wellness, comfort products (90-110 BPM)

### Sound Layering
- ASMR sound effect: 0-30% volume
- Background music: 50-70% volume
- Voiceover (optional): 80-100% volume

### Voiceover Timing
If using voiceover, keep it brief:
- Hook voiceover: 0-2 seconds ("Stop. You need this.")
- Benefit voiceover: 2-8 seconds (one key benefit only)
- CTA voiceover: Final 2 seconds (urgency, action)

## Common Mistakes & How to Fix Them

### Mistake 1: Unclear Product Showcase
**Problem:** Product is obscured, lighting is unflattering, angles are confusing.
**Fix:** Use Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield to generate clear, isolated product shots. Light from key/fill angles to show dimension. Include 3+ clear angles (front, side, detail). White background works for e-commerce.

### Mistake 2: Hook Doesn't Hook
**Problem:** First 2 seconds don't stop the scroll. Hook is generic or too slow.
**Fix:** Choose one of the 12 hook frameworks. Make it visual, not text-based. Add movement, color contrast, or emotion. Test: would YOU stop scrolling? If not, reframe.

### Mistake 3: Too Much Information
**Problem:** Text overlays cover the screen. Copy is longer than a tweet. Viewers feel overwhelmed.
**Fix:** One key message per 5 seconds. Max 5-8 words per overlay. Prioritize: benefit > feature > price. Let visuals do 70% of the work.

### Mistake 4: Lifestyle Context Missing
**Problem:** Product only shown in isolation. Viewers don't see themselves using it.
**Fix:** Include 30-40% of video in lifestyle/in-context shots. Show person wearing, using, or enjoying product in relatable setting. Lifestyle doesn't need to be aspirational—authentic relatable works.

### Mistake 5: CTA Is Weak or Missing
**Problem:** Video ends without clear next step. Viewers don't know how to buy.
**Fix:** Final 3 seconds must have clear CTA. Use action words: "Shop Now," "Get It Today," "Join Waitlist." Include price if not shown earlier. Add urgency: "Limited Time," "Only 5 Left," "48-Hour Sale."

### Mistake 6: Wrong Music Choice
**Problem:** Music doesn't match product category or platform vibe. Feels disjointed.
**Fix:** Match music BPM to energy level. Luxury products: 80-100 BPM. Social products: 130-150 BPM. Use platform-trending audio for TikTok. Ensure music license covers commercial use.

### Mistake 7: Cutting Too Fast or Too Slow
**Problem:** Cuts are jarring or video drags. Loses viewer attention.
**Fix:** Social media ads: 0.75-1.5 second cuts. YouTube: 2-3 second holds. Match cuts to music beat. Quick-cut at hook, slow down for lifestyle moment, quick-cut to CTA. Vary rhythm.

### Mistake 8: Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield Prompt Too Vague
**Problem:** Generated video doesn't match vision. Product renders wrong. Angles are unclear.
**Fix:** Be hyper-specific in prompt. Describe exact product details, color, size, lighting angle, camera movement. Use timestamps (0-2s, 2-8s, etc.). Example shot references help (e.g., "similar to Apple's product videos").

### Mistake 9: Ignoring Platform Specs
**Problem:** Video rendered 16:9 but TikTok cuts off sides. Text appears off-screen.
**Fix:** Always generate for exact platform aspect ratio (9:16 for TikTok/Reels, 1:1 for Instagram Feed, 16:9 for YouTube). Test on actual platform before publishing.

### Mistake 10: Product Doesn't Look Premium
**Problem:** Lighting is flat, colors are washed out. Product looks cheap.
**Fix:** Use Seedance 2.0 on Higgsfield's advanced lighting control. Specify "dramatic side lighting," "key light from top-left," "rim lighting to separate from background." Use color grading—warm for luxury, cool for tech, vibrant for youth products.
